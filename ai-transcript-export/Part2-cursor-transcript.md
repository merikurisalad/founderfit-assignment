### Part 1 — Outreach Campaign Frameworks (What the Pros Use)

Before diving into the tactical plan, here are four proven frameworks that real companies use to run outreach campaigns. These will shape how we structure your list-building and eventual outreach.

---

#### Framework 1: Predictable Revenue ("Cold Calling 2.0") — Aaron Ross

Developed at Salesforce, where it generated \$100M+ in recurring revenue. The core idea: **lead generation process, not more salespeople, drives growth.**

**The 5-step process:**
1. Build your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and target list
2. Run outbound email campaigns (50–100 emails/day, targeting ~10% response rate)
3. Qualify leads through discovery calls
4. "Sell the dream" — address pain points directly
5. Pass qualified leads to closers

**Key insight for FounderFit:** You don't cold-call founders. You send short, referral-oriented emails ("Who on your team handles X?") to create warmer paths in.

> **Source:** Ross, Aaron. *Predictable Revenue* (2011). Summary at [predictablerevenue.com/blog/15-minute-summary-of-predictable-revenue](https://predictablerevenue.com/blog/15-minute-summary-of-predictable-revenue/)

---

#### Framework 2: BASHO Email Framework — Jeff Hoffman

A hyper-personalization framework designed to reach hard-to-reach decision-makers. SaaStock founder Alexander Theuma reports **50–65% response rates** using this method.

**The 4 components:**
1. **Research** — Reference the prospect's specific achievements, press, or milestones
2. **Pain Point** — Frame a challenge as a strategic question (not a pitch)
3. **Value** — Present your solution tied directly to their pain
4. **Engagement** — Simple, low-friction CTA ("Worth a 15-min chat?")

**Key insight for FounderFit:** For a 50-person list, you can afford to be deeply personal. BASHO is the right framework at this scale — mass-blast is not.

> **Source:** Hoffman, Jeff. Basho Strategies Inc. Detailed breakdown at [close.com/blog/basho-email](https://close.com/blog/basho-email)

---

#### Framework 3: Signal-Driven ICP Definition (Modern GTM, 2025–2026)

Modern B2B teams have moved beyond static demographic profiles. The current best practice is a **signal-driven ICP** scored on four dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Pain** | Does the prospect have a problem you solve? |
| **Money** | Can they afford / justify the investment? |
| **Reachability** | Can you actually get to them? (email, social, warm intro) |
| **Fit** | Do their firmographics/technographics match your sweet spot? |

Teams also layer in **behavioral signals**: recent funding, hiring patterns, expansion announcements, and tech stack changes. Intent-driven strategies achieve **78% higher conversion** than static lists.

> **Source:** GTM Signal Studio, "Complete ICP Guide for GTM Success (2026)" — [gtmsignalstudio.com/complete-icp-guide-2026](https://gtmsignalstudio.com/complete-icp-guide-2026); SparkCo AI ICP Framework — [sparkco.ai/blog/build-ideal-customer-profile-icp-framework](https://sparkco.ai/blog/build-ideal-customer-profile-icp-framework)

#### Framework 4: Clay's Outbound Prospecting Playbook

Clay (the enrichment/automation tool) recommends a specific operational sequence for startups building prospect lists:

1. **Define ICP first** — triggers, personas, geography, company size (before touching any tool)
2. **Pull target accounts** — from LinkedIn, Crunchbase, CSV scrapes, directories
3. **Enrich at the company level first** — qualify the *company* before finding *people* (saves time)
4. **Pull and filter contacts** — aggressive filtering: no generic emails, no junior roles
5. **Create personalization variables** — AI-generated first lines based on hiring patterns, tech stack, press

**Key insight for FounderFit:** Enrich companies first, people second. Don't waste time finding the founder's email for a brand that doesn't actually qualify.

> **Source:** GTM Scaler, "How to Use Clay for B2B Outbound" — [gtm-scaler.com/how-to-use-clay-for-b2b-outbound-a-practical-playbook](https://gtm-scaler.com/how-to-use-clay-for-b2b-outbound-a-practical-playbook/); Clay University — [university.clay.com](https://university.clay.com/lessons/finding-companies-in-clay)

Not every "eco" brand is a fit. Here's a scoring rubric to qualify someone as a **sustainable fashion founder** worth reaching out to:

## 3-Touch Outreach Sequence: Instagram DM → LinkedIn → Email

---

### Sequence Strategy Overview

| | Touchpoint 1 | Touchpoint 2 | Touchpoint 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Channel** | Instagram DM | LinkedIn message | Email |
| **Timing** | Day 1 | Day 4–5 | Day 8–10 |
| **Tone** | Warm, casual, founder-to-founder | Professional but personal | Concise, value-forward |
| **Goal** | Start a conversation | Establish credibility | Create a clear reason to respond |

**Why this order:**
- **Instagram first** — This is where your prospects *live*. Early-stage sustainable fashion founders check DMs more than email. The platform lets you reference their actual products, posts, and stories, which makes personalization feel natural rather than forced. A DM also signals "I'm in your world" rather than "I'm selling you something."
- **LinkedIn second** — Acts as a credibility escalation. If they didn't respond on Instagram (or don't have an active IG), a LinkedIn message shows you're a real professional, not a bot. It also gives you room to reference their company journey, hiring, or press in a way that would feel awkward in a DM.
- **Email last** — The fallback for founders who aren't responsive on social, or whose contact info you only have via email. Email also allows for slightly longer messaging and is where founders are more accustomed to receiving partnership/business proposals.

**Key design principles across all three:**
- Every message can **stand alone** — no "following up on my DM" cross-references, since they may not see the other channels
- Each message uses a **different hook** so it doesn't feel like copy-paste across platforms
- Every CTA is **low-friction** — you're asking for a conversation, not a commitment
- Each message has a **[PERSONALIZATION]** placeholder — this is where BASHO-style research goes

---

### Touchpoint 1: Instagram DM (Day 1)

**Goal:** Break the ice. Show you're a real person who genuinely knows their work. Open a casual dialogue.

**Why Instagram:** This is where they're most comfortable and most likely to see your message. Referencing a specific product or post proves you're not mass-blasting.

---

**Message:**

> Hey [First Name]! I came across [Brand Name] through [specific post / collection / hashtag] — love what you're doing with [specific detail: e.g., "the deadstock denim line" / "your approach to transparent pricing" / "the zero-waste production process"].
>
> I'm working on something called FounderFit — we're building a platform specifically for early-stage founders in sustainable fashion who are navigating the hard parts of scaling (hiring, funding, staying true to your values while growing).
>
> Would love to hear what's been your biggest challenge this year — always trying to learn from founders who are actually doing the work. No pitch, just genuinely curious.

---

**Why this works:**
- **Opens with their work, not yours** — the first sentence is about them
- **Specific reference** — proves you've looked at their brand (the `[specific detail]` placeholder is critical — never send this without filling it in)
- **Positions FounderFit as relevant, not salesy** — one sentence, framed around their world
- **CTA is a question, not an ask** — "what's been your biggest challenge" invites a real conversation and gives you intel for the next touchpoint
- **"No pitch, just genuinely curious"** — disarms the sales reflex

### The Diagnostic Framework (When Something Isn't Working)

If your numbers are low, the problem is always in one of four places. Check in this order:

| **Channel** | One channel has 0% response, others are fine | Drop the dead channel, reallocate to the one that works. |
| **Message** | People open/see but don't reply | Your hook or CTA isn't resonating. Test a different opening line or a softer ask. |
| **Timing** | Decent response rate but no meetings | You're reaching them at the wrong moment. Add more trigger-event filtering (just launched, just hired, just got press). |
